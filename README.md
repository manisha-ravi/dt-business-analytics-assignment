# Business Analytics Assignment – Target Company Research

##  Applicant Note
I approached this assignment with the intention of thinking like a Business Analyst, not just generating answers. I did use AI as a support tool, but I made sure to question and refine the outputs instead of directly accepting them.

---

##  1. Company Selected
Zomato Ltd.

---

##  2. Objective
The objective of this analysis is to understand Zomato’s business model, identify key operational challenges, and suggest improvements using a structured business analytics approach.

---

##  3. Business Model Overview
Zomato operates as a platform connecting customers, restaurants, and delivery partners.

Its main services include:
- Food delivery  
- Restaurant discovery  
- Quick commerce (Blinkit)  

### Revenue Streams:
- Delivery fees charged to customers  
- Commission from restaurants  
- Advertisements and promotions  
- Subscription services (Zomato Gold)  

---

##  4. Problem Statement
While Zomato has grown significantly, it still struggles with profitability and operational efficiency.

From my understanding, the key problems are:
- High delivery costs  
- Strong competition (especially from Swiggy)  
- Customer dissatisfaction due to delays or pricing  
- Heavy dependency on discounts  

These problems directly affect both user experience and long-term sustainability.

---

##  5. Hypothesis
If Zomato improves delivery efficiency and provides more personalized user experiences, it can increase customer retention and reduce unnecessary costs, ultimately improving profitability.

---

##  6. Analysis Approach

If I had access to data, I would approach the problem in the following way:

### Step 1: User Behavior Analysis
- Identify repeat vs one-time users  
- Analyze frequency of orders  
- Check at what point users stop ordering  

### Step 2: Delivery Performance Analysis
- Track delivery times across locations  
- Identify peak-hour delays  
- Study cancellation patterns  

### Step 3: Pricing & Discount Analysis
- Compare orders with and without discounts  
- Identify if discounts bring long-term users or just short-term spikes  

I initially tried asking AI for metrics, but many suggestions were generic, so I focused only on practical and relevant ones.

---

##  7. Insights (Based on Logical Reasoning)

- Orders are likely higher during weekends and evenings, which may cause delivery delays  
- Longer delivery times can lead to cancellations and reduced trust  
- Discounts may increase orders but do not always ensure customer loyalty  
- A small group of repeat users probably contributes a major share of revenue  

These are not exact data-backed insights but are based on how such platforms typically operate.

---

##  8. Recommendations

### 1. Improve Delivery Efficiency
Use better route optimization and delivery partner allocation to reduce delays.

**Why:** Faster delivery improves customer satisfaction and retention.

---

### 2. Personalized Recommendations
Suggest restaurants based on past orders and preferences.

**Why:** Makes ordering easier and increases repeat usage.

---

### 3. Smarter Discount Strategy
Offer targeted discounts instead of giving them to all users.

**Why:** Reduces unnecessary spending while still retaining valuable users.

---

### 4. Focus on Repeat Customers
Introduce loyalty programs or rewards.

**Why:** Retaining existing customers is cheaper than acquiring new ones.

---

##  9. Expected Impact

- Reduced delivery delays  
- Better customer retention  
- Lower operational costs  
- Improved profitability over time  

---

##  10. Guardrails Against AI Hallucination

While using AI, I followed some precautions:

- Avoided using any specific numbers unless I was sure  
- Cross-checked basic information from Zomato’s website  
- Ignored overly confident or vague AI responses  
- Focused more on logic than generated content  

---

##  11. Where I Disagreed with AI

In some cases, AI suggested very broad ideas like “expand to new markets” or “increase marketing spend.”

I did not include those because:
- They are generic and not specific to the problem  
- They do not directly address operational inefficiencies  

Instead, I focused on practical improvements like delivery optimization and retention.

---

##  12. Negative Prompting Used

While interacting with AI, I used prompts like:

- “Do not generate assumptions without basis”  
- “Avoid generic business advice”  
- “Focus on realistic Indian market conditions”  

This helped in getting more usable outputs.
